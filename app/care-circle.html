12 Sep
Chris 9/12 update

Radiation complete

I have completed the radiation treatments and have managed to keep my weight at 200. I have no taste and no appetite but my voice is still working. A bit of random coughing and still a lot of mucus. The thing that sucks the most is the loss of taste and no desire to eat.

So now it is a waiting game to see when I start to feel back to normal (well for me anyway), there are some other side effects that are a bit frustrating.

Week 3 down and food has become a bit of a challenge. No fun.

I feel good otherwise some throat soreness and coughing. Voice is still strong.

22 Aug
Chris 8/22 update

Week 2 — started losing taste

Week 2 down and I can easily say this seriously sucks. Started to lose taste the last couple of days.

On a positive note, I feel good otherwise. I need a good nights sleep or I am worthless after radiation.

They have a cancer specific blood test that they ran before and after my surgery and it was confirmed that they did get all the cancer which is good news. The whole reason for doing the radiation is in case there was some microscopic cells left behind which could reignite — my doctor said it's a 50-50 shot if they are there or not.

15 Aug
Chris 8/15 update

Week 1 of radiation done

Well week one down and I feel some tenseness in my neck, but other than that I feel pretty much the same. Voice is a bit better since Monday.

The biggest pain in the ass of radiation so far is the going to New Haven every day. But I've been taking the bike, so that probably saves me about 15 minutes a day in travel time and another 20 minutes not having to jockey for a parking spot. So at least I got that going for me :)

Day 1 of radiation

Day 1 not too bad, the mask is super tight and uncomfortable but I am only in there like 5 to 10 minutes. Need to figure out timing to get there, park, get checked in etc.

I asked the woman at the front desk "As I am coming everyday for 5 weeks isn't there like a card I could use as a speed pass or something to bypass checking in when I walk in to the building?" Not surprisingly she said no, she said that was the speed pass haha :)

23 Jul
Chris 7/23 update

Pathology results

Got some good news re pathology. Here it is in a nutshell:

I have an early-stage HPV-positive oropharyngeal cancer. The tumor was completely removed with clear margins. There is limited lymph node involvement with no evidence of spread beyond the nodes. This type of cancer typically responds very well to treatment, and Stage I has an excellent survival rate.

Still in a ton of pain but I have been talking too much — the pain sucks but not being able to speak my mind is incredibly frustrating. I smartened up and started using a straw for protein shake/coffee/water as I can direct it to the right side of my throat.

18 Jul
Chris 7/18 update

I am home!

I am home! Swallowing is very difficult. Diet restrictions not too bad, soft foods, but oatmeal is even hard to eat.

Surgery was 7/16 — Dr. Roche performed the excision of the left neck mass, left neck dissection, direct laryngoscopy and biopsy. In the hospital 3 nights.

26 Jun
Chris Where it started

Starting this page

I decided to put this together for people that want to know what is happening.

On May 19, I woke up to a large baseball sized lump on the left side of my neck so I went to the emergency room. Nine hours later after a 4 inch needle stuck in my throat to drain what they thought was a cleft cyst, they sent me home.

On June 6, it was an appointment with a doctor in New Haven and they recommended surgery to go in and remove the cyst or to see what was going on in there. Surgery scheduled on June 23. Within those two weeks, the thing on my neck had gone down so much that I almost didn't do the surgery because it was unnoticeable. It appears it was the right decision based on their findings.

Post-op diagnosis: Squamous cell carcinoma left neck, squamous cell carcinoma left tonsil. Surgeon: Dr. Ansley Roche.
