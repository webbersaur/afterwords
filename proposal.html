The Problem

When a patient hears the word "cancer," everything else fades out. Critical details about diagnosis, treatment plans, and next steps are lost in the moment. Then they're overwhelmed by dozens of well-meaning calls and texts — adding stress during the hardest time of their life.

"When my doctor called to tell me I had cancer, I asked if I could record it — because I knew I wouldn't remember a word. That recording changed everything. I could go back, process it, and share it clearly with the people who needed to know."

— Chris Hauman, cancer survivor & AfterWords founder

The Solution

AfterWords is a patient-first web app that solves both problems in one experience:

🎙 Visit Recap

Patients record or upload their doctor visits. AI transcribes the conversation and generates a structured summary — diagnosis, treatment plan, action items, and follow-up questions — in plain language they can review at their own pace.

👥 Care Circle

A private update page where patients (or caregivers) post updates about their journey. Friends and family follow along and send messages of support — no more fielding dozens of individual calls and texts.
